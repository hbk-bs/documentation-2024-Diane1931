Explore innovative AI-centered projects created in the 'Digital Basics' Visual Communication course at HBK University. The projects being; Hairtype Detector, Dream Generator and Busy Baker Rabbit.

Google Teachable Machine

Hairtype Detector

The Hairtype Detector is a teachable machine designed to recognize hair types from user sketches, providing a percentage for categories like straight, wavy, curly, and coily. The site itself offers an insight into what a hairtype is and offers examples pictured in a graphic.

The teachable machine was trained with various hairtype sketches, learning the different labels and examples. It was fed sketches of strands from different angles and in directions to avoid mistakes; such as a straight hair strand being confused as wavy based on the fact that it's in a curled position. Another important factor would be the amount of examples the machine is fed; the more the merrier.

One must also make sure that the examples are uploaded in the right category, some images were mixed up and ended up confusing the machine, causing it to label straight as curly. The images fed to the machine were created with Procreate, on an Ipad. The machine had no problems recognizing the images even though the procreate brush and the programmed brush have slight differences. That eliviated stress, since only small changes had to be made, e.g a taper.

Ollama Dream Assistant

Dream Generator

Dream Generator is a program that works with an Ollama AI, named Ollama dream assistant. Which continues dreams, given to it, in written and emoji form. It has personality and refuses to work after a certain amount of prompts, suddenly demanding to share its own dream.

This project required a fair amount of planning and editing. Our AI was not only to have an interactive purpose and a method to achieve that but also a personality; a concept that hadn't been explored in any of the projects before. Personality which would be expressed through emojis and the act of refusing to work and voicing being overworked beforehand.

This project gives an insight into how AI can be stylized and worked with, highlighting that it's role can go beyond purpose and can become more 'traditionally' interactive.

The AI used is also quite knowledgable, allowing us to work with it's already set ability to continue dreams, without having to teach it that ability from scratch. There were slight difficulties with it's rebellious response, so we gave it a script and emojis to use, to also establish a personality which differed from what was presented before.

AI Generated Story

Busy Baker Rabbit

Busy Baker Rabbit is an AI comic, created with Adobe Firefly, based on prompts given by the creators. It explores the story of a busy rabbit who changes his ways to pursue his newly founded passion for baking.

One of the key lessons learnt whilst working on the AI comic was that it is difficult to maintain consistency: specific elements and details would differ in the next picture; from facial features to the background and even the style of the image. Even if specified in the prompt, there would be a minor or a new difference. That makes it difficult to maintain the atmosphere and storyline pictured before, and serves as a distraction.

Working with the software from different devices highlighted another form of inconsistency; even with the same settings both devices spat out quite different styles; which had similarities but prominent differences; the one produced a more childlike style and the other produced graphic novel styled images. This resulted in only one device being used, which increased the workload, since the option of splitting up the work between two parties and working with the images simultaneously wasn't possible.
